The story how I got in contact with Ruby

Long story short: Here is the TL;DR

Chapter I: The adventure begins

My first programming experience was when I was 14 years old and participating in a programming course at our school. Though the teacher was an old school programmer and we worked on outdated computers with QBasic I got immediately hooked. In my free time I sat together with a friend and we started to write our own games. We worked on simple games like tick-tack-toe and wild text-adventures up to more sophisticated games with ASCII graphics and 8-bit music.

Chapter II: The road to the kingdom

Soon I became aware that QBasic is very outdated and not the future. So I started to look into other languages like Java and VisualBasic. Somehow I could not get comfortable with Java and settled with Microsoft's VisualBasic. The syntax was very similar to what I already knew and the visual editor was very impressive. So I started to write Windows programs for the school (Yearbook photo application) and myself (notepad, chats, keygens, etc).

Chapter III: Arriving at the capital

And then I found my way to the future; the Internet...
So I started to read some tutorials about HTML, CSS and PHP, wrote some Diablo II and Warcraft 3 fan pages and started my apprenticeship as a software engineer at one of the biggest retail stores in Switzerland. In school and at work I they used Java and so I learned for the first time a programming language without teaching myself.
Soon I exceeded the knowledge of my superior and teacher and got bored. Thanks to a lot of free time at work and projects at school I kept working with PHP and started to write a full browser game with the help of a graphic enthusiastic friend.

Chapter IV: Meeting the princess

In our computer science class at school we worked mostly on projects in small teams. There I met an other enthusiastic programmer and we ended up working mostly together. One day he told me about the new thing called Rails, a framework for web development based on the programming language Ruby.
At first a bit skeptical, after some time reading about it I tried it by implementing my first side project.
And I was immediately hooked...

Chapter V: Entering the castle

There was something magical happening. Several things I missed in other languages and frameworks where there: A readable syntax, a test framework that was actually usable, scaffolding, meta programming (poof, blew my mind), etc. A lot of concepts I've never heard before like TDD, REST and duck typing made a lot of sense to me.
I kept working with Ruby and Rails for side projects but was bound to Java and ABAP at work. I tried to get more concepts from Rails into the other languages and environments but had to recognize that it was not possible: The limits were set by the languages and how they restricted the developer.

Chapter V: The wedding

At one point I could not take it anymore. I felt like stuck in a prison. I quit my job, started my own software company and worked for several Ruby companies. I wouldn't change a thing.

And they all lived happily ever after.

The end.

TL;DR: In short...

A friend introduced my to Rails in 2007 and I got hooked immediately by the concepts of the framework.
Later I realised that the beauty and simplicity of Rails was heavily dependent of its language Ruby. These are the things that made me fall in love with Ruby and made me stick with it till now:

  • Human readable syntax
  • Duck typing
  • Meta programming
  • Usable test framework
  • Awesome community
  • Matz
